RCC 2018: Listen

Throughout our history, visionaries have inspired us all to see the world in a new way. Though their impact is far reaching, the beginning always started with a place. A place to gather, a place to create. A safe haven where the seeds of progress are sown. "Progress sounds like the rhythm of change" Listen explores the progress of life, love and art, through the poignant poetry of Charlie Schmidt. "When doubters build walls, we tear down barriers with believers of infinite possibilities" Now more than ever, it’s important for us to hear the call of progress, to Listen...

Mandarins 2017:
Inside the Ink

2017's Inside the Ink is a dive into the schizophrenic mind. It's a collection of imagery around the subject of mental illness and depression. Large ink pens suggest the hand-drawn ink blots by Rorschach himself. He drew them to look random, conflicted and ambiguous, so as to draw out emotions and commentary by subjects who were being tested for schizophrenia. Two contrasting ideas cultivate throughout the show: the sane and the insane.

RCC 2017:
Silence of the Heavens

Motionless. The perfect stillness of the red dragonfly; resting above the shimmering water. Waiting... 2017 RCC presents Silence of the Heavens. Inspired by Daisaku Ikeda's Poetry by Water, and brought to life with the breathtaking score of Maurice Ravel's Trio in A Minor and the brilliant performers of RCC. The Silence of the Heavens is a snapshot in time, a fleeting moment when the clouds part to reveal a ray of sunlight, giving way to one of the most iconic pieces of nature, associated with purity, rebirth, and divinity.

Pacific Crest 2016:
The Union

This is the story of the first transcontinental railroad; the greatest, most daring engineering effort the country had yet seen. Imagine the task. The idea was to span the west with iron rails and to build a railroad across two-thirds of the continent on some of the most difficult terrain on earth. Not in all that distance, not in 1,700 miles, was there a single settlement of any appreciable size. The railroad would join what essentially were two different countries: California and back East. They called it a work of giants. But like all great stories, it’s about the people. Using current music and unique textures, this five-part program will explore a modernized twist on one of America’s biggest historical accomplishments. Not only did the railroad connect the East and West, it connected people and created a Union, one spike at a time.

Pacific Crest 2015:
The Catalyst

A mysterious artifact has arrived. We aren’t sure where it came from or why it is even here. We call it The Catalyst. A catalyst is an agent that provokes or speeds significant change or action; as the performers react to it, the music changes from dark and aggressive to light and regal. And like a chemical catalyst, this one also changes as it reacts to the performers. The production is developed around the musical works of Prokofiev, Uematsu, and Ticheli, which reflect the reaction initiated by The Catalyst as the show progresses from chaos and conflict to an optimistic and consonant resolution.
