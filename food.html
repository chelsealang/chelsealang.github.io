food spots

Listed in Alphabetical Order: All photos are taken by me!

Korean BBQ Recommendations: Hae Jang Chon, Moodaepoo (Thirsty Cow), Gram's BBQ, Quarter's, Oo-kook
Boba Recommendations: Omomo Tea Shoppe, Happy Lemon, Half & Half, The Alley, Ding Tea, Sharetea

Blue Bowl Superfoods
(Huntington Beach, CA)

Hands down, one of the best places for acai bowls. After having acai bowls from various places, Blue Bowl just doesn’t compare to the rest. I can’t exactly remember what I got in this photo, but I think I got acai for my base and hemp for granola! They also have the blue bowl chia as an option for the base (which I’ve had) and it’s equally as delicious! In addition, toppings are unlimited, so you can go all out!

Brodard Restaurant (Fountain Valley, CA)

Although they also have various other vietnamese dishes here, this is the go-to place for grilled pork spring rolls. They also have various other vietnamese dishes here. As for their spring rolls though, I think the best part is the type of grilled pork they have, along with the crispy egg roll skin. But I also can’t forget about the sauce! Just so many good things about it, along with the fact it’s the perfect to-go meal.

Butaton Ramen
(Garden Grove, CA)

I’ve only been here once to try it out, and was surprised at how good it was! I had the butaton ramen, but I think they’re pretty well known for having good vegetarian ramen. Broth was very rich and creamy, but I still would rank this lower than Kitakata & HiroNori.

Cauldron Ice Cream (Costa Mesa, CA)

They’re known for their Puffle Cone, inspired by the popular Hong Kong street snack. They use liquid nitrogen to create their handmade ice cream as well. I’m in love with their flavor, Sun Moon & Stars, which is a creamy milk tea ice cream with notes of jasmine, oolong, and green tea. Sometimes, they also make pretty rose designs with the ice cream. Although it is very delicious and a trendy spot, it can be quite expensive where a puffle cone with a scoop of ice cream costs almost $7.

Coco Ichibanya
(Irvine, CA)

Best japanese curry in California. If this is your first time, I highly recommend the simple chicken/pork cutlet curry. If you would like, you can get the curry with the omelette too. I also highly recommend the garlic nan as well! You can choose the level of spice and add more rice if you’d like. They charge a little extra for extra spice but I’ve been told it’s worth it. The wait can get pretty long, so use the Yelp check in system! Diamond Jamboree has many other food spots and HMart if you want to wander around and kill time.

Corn Man
(Los Angeles, CA)

The best elotes in Los Angeles. Check is Twitter @the_CornStation for their schedules. He is one of the most hardworking, light-hearted men I’ve personally ever interacted with. He is also extremely generous with the toppings - cheese, mayo, butter, and freshly squeezed lime juice. Chili powder is optional. You can order the bowl or the cob. My friends usually prefer the bowl because it’s cleaner to eat, of course. They’re both $2.50 each! The waits can get pretty long, but it’s so worth it!

DaDa Shabu Shabu Buffet (Irvine, CA)

As a self-proclaimed shabu enthusiast and Korean food junkie, everything about this place fills my heart with joy. This is one of the best AYCE shabu-shabu places in all of OC, second to Shabuya. :) Prior to COVID-19, they had a self service buffet area for the veggies, fried rice, fried chicken, chow mein, all the essentials! Usually, you would order the meat from the server. They have a very wide range of selection of sauces, seafood, veggies, and meat.

Din Tai Fung
(Costa Mesa, CA)

This is a classic, but one I go to very rarely just because of how expensive it is. However, everything about it is still very good. They’re known for the pork xiao long bao or XLB. Other items I highly recommend are the shrimp and pork wontons in spicy sauce, noodles with sesame sauce, and their green beans. As everyone may know, wait times are ridiculous. Although it’s very expensive and wait times are long, you know it’s still worth it and very crowded.

Eggslut
(Los Angeles, CA)

I think this place is more of a trendy popular food spot at Grand Central Market in Los Angeles. The reason I say that is it isn’t anything too special and quite pricey. If you’re looking for an expensive bacon, egg, and cheese breakfast sandwich, you’ll find that here!

Glee's Donuts & Burgers
(Fountain Valley, CA)

This was one of my old time favorites (when it was still named Kelly’s under different owners). Since then, all prices have spiked quite a bit. I’ve never gotten their burgers - only their breakfast burritos, specifically the bacon, egg, and cheese burrito. I also recommend their orange juice and donuts.

Good Mong Kok Bakery (San Francisco, CA)

This place is a delicious cantonese dim sum take-out spot. At very reasonable prices too! I’d say the only downside is their lines can get long. This place gave me a chance to practice my cantonese out in the real world (lol). My favorites are siu mai, har gow, char siu bao, and dan tat! I highly recommend checking out this place for inexpensive and good quality dim sum!

Hakata Ikkousha
(Irvine, CA)

This is one of my boyfriend’s favorite ramen spots, so I guess I’ll include it here. If you’re looking for a hakata style ramen, I’d recommend this place over shin sen gumi. My recommendation is the black tonkotsu ramen. His recommendation is the black devil ramen, a mix of god fire ramen and black tonkotsu ramen. The broth is amazing…

Happy Lemon (Irvine, CA)

They’re known for their salted cheese drinks, so I’d either recommend green or black tea with salted cheese. Sometimes, they’ll top your salted cheese with some matcha. Most sunmerry bakeries also have happy lemon in their stores, so it’s a great opportunity to enjoy your drink with some baked goods! If you have never had a tea with salted cheese drink, you’re in for a treat!

HiroNori Ramen
(Irvine, CA)

Probably my mom’s favorite and one of my favorite ramen spots. It’s pretty busy during weekends, with about hour long waits. I honestly can’t pinpoint what exactly it is, but this seriously hits the spot every time. It’s probably the fact that everything is fresh - fresh cut noodles, handmade broth and sauce. But you’re genuinely missing out if you have not been. My go-to order is the tonkotsu ramen with thick noodles and an egg, along with an order of chicken karage on the side. Also don’t forget to ask for some complimentary house spicy sauce and garlic!

In N' Out
(Huntington Beach, CA)

This is a classic in California, but I have to include this since I included Shake Shack here. I feel like I don’t really have to explain - it never gets old, and if you haven’t been, you’re missing out. I am definitely craving some animal style fries right now...
