CPR Guide Glove

This assistive device was developed in the form of a glove to help users optimize the quality and effectiveness of manual chest compressions in accordance with CPR guidelines. It is designed to encourage bystander intervention by providing the tools and confidence necessary to act under pressure. The device provides real-time visual feedback on the depth of chest compressions and contains a metronome to help maintain a constant rate of 100 bpm.

Pembroza: Biosimilar to Merck's Keytruda

A biosimilar is a biologic medical product (also known as biologic) highly similar to another already approved biological medicine (the 'reference medicine'). Biosimilars are approved according to the same standards of pharmaceutical quality, safety and efficacy that apply to all biological medicines. My team and I outlined the process in developing a biosimilar to Merck's Keytruda or Pembrolizumab.

Cellular Regulation of NF-kB

The NF-kB signaling pathway is important for regulating processes of immune and inflammatory responses. NF-kB acts as a transcription factor and induces the expression of various pro-inflammatory genes, including those encoding cytokines and chemokines. Misregulation of NF-kB activity has been identified as a major culprit of chronic inflammatory diseases and cancer. Within the NF-kB signaling pathway, there is a canonical and non-canonical pathway. The canonical pathway relies on inducible degradation of IKBs leading to translocation of the p50/RelA dimer into the nucleus. To better understand the physiological system, a block diagram and the mathematical relationship of its components are designed to highlight the mechanisms of the canonical NF-kB pathway, as well as its regulation through negative feedback.

MedAlarm

MedAlarm is a medication dispenser equipped with an alarm and countdown timer that is set by the user. When the timer is up, the dispenser will sound an alarm until the medication is properly dispensed to the user. After the medication has been taken from the device, the timer will automatically reset to the previous time set. This project won 2nd place overall at BioHack 2018.

Anti-Phylax Pen

Allergies affect common people every single day ranging from minor to severe. In fact, according to a recent study done by Food Allergy Research and Education, over 200,000 people go to the emergency room to seek medical help for allergic reactions to food. These reactions include anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that occurs quickly and abruptly from a person being exposed to allergens. As a result, people have been using an auto-injector device called the EpiPen, which is filled with a 0.3 mg shot of epinephrine intended to treat anaphylaxis. The Anti-Phylax Pen is designed to address the issues EpiPen users have, such as its bulky size, high costs, and unnecessary bruising.
